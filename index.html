IIT BOMBAY OPERATION RAHAT

2018

Being a partaker in one of Asia's largest tech fest hosted by IIT Bombay, Team SCRO was one of the contestants in the Drone Making Challenge. Visualising and Implementing the framework which was entirely 3D printed, the printing was done by using ABS(Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene) which efficiently handles the heat dissipated from the motors while the drone manoeuvres. Widely appreciated, the elephant in the room had many benefits such as the image processing camera, through which the video could be assessed on mobile phones or at ground stations. Foldable, lightweight, and most importantly economical, the proposition presented by the team grabbed everybody’s attention. Preserved as one of the proudest moments for Team SCRO to showcase their innovation at an extravagant technology fest, the experience was unmatchable!

HOVERBIKE

2019

In simple words, a hoverbike is a combination of a motorcycle and a drone. This e-VTOL does not need a runway, can take off and land vertically. It is easy to operate and has huge applications Team SCRO in collaboration with a start-up Creative Winds designed and fabricated the prototype for India’s first Hoverbike designed by students. The prototype can lift a person up to 60 kgs and has 10 mins of flight time. The fabrication took place in SRM IST’s SIIC Fablab and the prototype was completed in just three months.

MEDICAL DRONE

2019

The lack of resources and professionals in rural or distant areas makes it hard for residents to get medical help. This can have dire consequences especially in the case of emergencies. In such situations, we can use the medical drone to carry the equipment to the right place. This will make the procedure easier and more convenient. The drone is customized to carry a medical kit, an automated external defibrillator and a camera that can be used in case of an emergency.

AGRICULTURE DRONE

2020

Agriculture drone is an autonomous drone used to monitor the crops. It does a multispectral analysis for the following tasks:
1.Identify pests, diseases and weeds and optimize pesticide usage.
2.Refine fertilization by detecting nutrient deficiencies.
3.Help with land management and determine spacing issues.
4.Control crop irrigation by identifying areas where water stress is suspected.
5.View damage to crops from farm machinery and make necessary repairs.
6.Survey fencing and farm buildings.
7.Monitor livestock using thermal cameras at night.
Airborne cameras can capture data from the infrared as well as the visual spectrum to differentiate between healthy and distressed plants in a way that can’t be seen with the naked eye. A drone can survey a crop every week, every day, or even every hour.

SEEDBOMBER

2020

The team has developed the seed bomber drone to airdrop seed balls anywhere suitable for the species and plant more trees in a larger area in less time. This makes things easier and reduces the human effort for maximum results. A seed ball (or seed bomb) has a ‘pre-planted’ seed that has been wrapped in soil materials, usually clay and manure.
The project runs in 2 phases:
1 st phase: A drone carrying a box (20*20) containing seed bombs is used. The box has a zigzag wedged system which allows the bombs to fall in a controlled manner instead of dropping all the bombs together.
2nd phase: Two drones are used. The 1st one checks the topography and marks the suitable area for bombing the seed using multispectral analysis. The 2nd drone goes to the locations and drops the seed bomb. The dropping system is modified using a servo and piston mechanism to drop 1-2 bombs at a time, allowing us to drop seed bombs in larger areas.

ANTIDRONE

2020

Drone has revolutionized the field of technology. As these small vehicles can be built easily and be used by everyone, restricting and keeping track of them and putting them down has become necessary. To monitor this, we built an Anti-Drone System which detects the drone and tracks it. Calculating the parameters such as number of Drones, speed of the Drone, distance from the camera, height from the Ground is the additional aspect this project covers.
